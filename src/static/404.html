"The School Day Drag"

The sun isn't up, but they want me to be,
Why can't they just let me stay in my dreams?

Hours crawl by, it's a terrible trick.
Math is confusing, history's a bore,
Do I really need to know what's come before?

Teachers drone on like robots on repeat,
Assignments pile up—there’s no time to breathe.

I’m sinking deep in this never-ending soup.

And don't get me started on those cafeteria folks.
We wait all day for that freedom bell,

They say it’s for learning, to help me succeed,
But all I want is some freedom, a little less speed.
Because school feels like prison, with no escape hatch,
Just waiting for weekends—the ultimate catch.

So here’s to the books, and the tests that I’ll fail,
To waking up early, and feeling so frail.
I’ll get through it, somehow, I guess,
But right now, school? It’s just a mess.
